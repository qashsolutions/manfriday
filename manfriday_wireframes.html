Scaling Monosemanticity: Extracting Interpretable Features from Claude
compiled from simonwillison.net 2 days ago concept: attention mechanism

This paper presents a method for extracting interpretable features from large language models using sparse autoencoders. The work builds directly on earlier research into monosemanticity and demonstrates that features can be identified at scale across Claude Sonnet.

Key findings

The researchers identified millions of features including abstract concepts, multimodal representations, and safety-relevant features. Crucially, many features generalise across languages and modalities — suggesting a degree of representational universality.

Connection to your wiki

This article connects to your existing notes on mixture of experts routing mechanisms and constitutional AI oversight. See also: sparse attention in MoE.
